What is Bee Decline?

In many industrialised countries around the world bees are struggling to thrive. The large decline in bee populations has been often referred to as Colony Collapse Disorder (CCD), a sudden and mysterious disappearance of worker bees within a honeybee hive causing the colony to collapse.. The cause of these sudden disappearances has been disputed among professionals and researchers alike, but many have come to understand the problem as systemic. There are many factors that contribute to bees inability to thrive. Habitat loss, industrial practices, pesticide and fungicide use have all been blamed along with many others.

Why is Bee Decline Important?

The pollination services provided by bees ensure our agricultural systems ability to produce the crops we rely on to feed the planet. According to the FDA, bee pollination is responsible for more than $15 billion in increased crop value each year, and provides one in every three mouthfuls of food we eat. If bees continue to stuggle, we would face rising food prices, nutritional discrepancies, and potentially food shortages around the world.

Agriculture and economics aside, bees play a key role in maintaining healthy, biodiverse ecologies as keystone species. Pollination supports a large proportion of plant varieties to survive and thrive.

Is Bee Decline Unusual?

Although this is not the first time in history beekeepers have experienced unexplained losses, the scale of modern bee decline is unprecedented. What Causes Bee Decline?

Unfortunately, the proven causes of modern bee decline remain mostly a mystery. Scientists and beekeepers outline a variety of potential causes, with many arguing that a combination of multiple stress factors are causing bee decline. It is the belief of the Open Source Beehives team that industrialised agricultural practices, such as pesticide use, migratory beekeeping, and extensive monocultures are putting bees and other pollinators at risk. By gathering sensor data, our project aims to identify and prove what some of these specific causes may be.
