4. Assembly

Box Window

1. Take each Box Front and place it flat on the a bench surface. You will need to have laser cut or milled the window panes from 3mm Plexiglass or similar material. Then tack in the window planes to secure them in place.

Quilt Frame

Note: Fold over the material and tack a second time for extra strength.

2. Take the Quilt Frame and place it with the rebate up. Then cut a hessian or affia material to fit the outer perimeter of the entire quilt frame. Carefully staple or tack the material to be taut into the rebate.

Base Plinth

Note: Place the base front with the space for the entrance block on the exterior.

3. Stand both Base Sides upright with the channel on the inside. Locate the Base Front to the end where the channels are closed and the base back to where they are open.

Landing/Floor

4. Slide the Landing Floor through the back until it is flush with the front side wall. No gaps.

Trap Door

5. Add the Trap Door to the top of the Landing Floor. Make sure that it is tight and flush with the top.

Window Box

6. Use the base as a guide to position the Front and Back Box Sides, making sure the grooves are on the inside. Then slide the side pieces into the grooves. Make sure the channels cut into the side pieces are facing out.

Box Rails

7. Add the Box handles from the back.

Box Locks

8. Push the Box Locks into the front of the box handles to lock them in place. This should be a tight fit!

Window Shutter

9. Push the window shutter into the front of the box.

Roof and Quilt Box

10. Take one Roof Gable end and slot in the Roof Side Panels and the Quilt Frame. Place the Quilt Frame with the hessian cloth upward.

Quilt Cap

Note: you should fill the quilt box with large chip sawdust at a later point.

11. Add the other Gable end and place the Quilt Cap onto the Roof Side Panels.

Roof Panels

12. Push both Roof Side Panels onto the gable ends making a tight fit with no over lap that could block the beam.

Roof Beam

13. Slide the roof beam over the top of the roof panels and through the sockets of the Roof Gabel ends.

Removable Parts

Note: you can now remove the Quilt Cap and fill the space beneith with large chip sawdust or any other natural insulation.

14. Stack the boxes and add eight bars to each of them.

15. Add the Entrance Block, firstly with the larger gap down to prepare for installing your bees.

Trap Door/Varroa Draw

Note: The Landing Floor can be pulled out and removed at any time. This will allow you to remove detritus and inspect for Varroa mite.

You may wish to remove the Trap Door and install a Varroa Mesh in its place, this adaption will serve well for the bees as the Varroa can then be collected and removed for analysis.
